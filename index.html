    Fake News Classification & Analysis

    The analysis of the FakeNewsNet dataset using K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) reveals that the model achieved its highest validation accuracy of 79% with n_neighbors set to 5. However, the accuracy slightly decreased around n_neighbors 17 before increasing again. The model successfully exceeded the required accuracy threshold of 80%, indicating its effectiveness in classifying news articles as fake or real based on various features. Notably, tweet_num, title length, and news_url_length are important features for the KNN model. The analysis also highlights that certain popular websites, such as Wikipedia, play a significant role in spreading both real and fake news, emphasizing the need for caution when trusting news sources and the impact of public opinion on their credibility.

    Energy Efficiency Analysis

    The kNN regression model provides important insights for optimizing energy efficiency in building heating and cooling loads. It identifies key features influencing these loads through correlation analysis, showing a slight improvement when adding more attributes. The model performs best with five features and achieves high accuracy when n_neighbor is 22. Importantly, it doesn't overfit, suggesting generalizability to new data. Overall, this model offers valuable information to improve energy consumption in the face of climate challenges, especially in the rapidly developing Philippines, where prioritizing energy efficiency is crucial for sustainable growth and development.
